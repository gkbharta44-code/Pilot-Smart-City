Assumptions & scope
  • 400 cities, $306.25B each, built in 5 batches of 80 cities, one batch every 5 years across 25 years.
  • Regional split: India 200 (Batches 1–3), Southeast Asia 120 (Batches 3–4), Gulf 80 (Batch 5) — an illustrative sequencing choice, not a confirmed market allocation.
  • 100% debt-financed via SBLC (sovereign-guarantee-backed), 3% pa interest, pooled across all batches — revenue from every operating city is swept to service the combined balance each year, which is what compresses full repayment from ~50 years (standalone-per-batch) to Year 27.
  • Rental revenue begins the year each batch completes, at 10% pa of that batch's invested capital, held flat (no growth modeled).
  • No construction contingency, land cost, or operating expense is modeled — this is a simplified illustrative payoff schedule, not a bankable term sheet.
  • This capital scale (~$4.9T/yr average draw) is still large relative to realistic global SBLC/MTN issuance capacity (see prior capital-market ceiling analysis) — treat as an aspirational upper-bound pilot, not a currently fundable plan.
