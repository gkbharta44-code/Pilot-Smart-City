Phase 1: Global Foothold — 195-Country Pilot City Sizing

Establish presence in every country first, sized to what each can realistically support — full-scale buildout follows as Phase 2

Phase 1 — Now
Global Foothold. One right-sized pilot city in each of 195 countries, sized to that country's own realistic sovereign guarantee capacity (capped at 3% of GDP). Total footprint: $2.904T, 47,411 acres, 23.7M residents. The objective here is breadth and proof — a real, financeable presence on every continent — not scale.
Phase 2 — Concurrent
Full-Scale Buildout. The complete 2,200-smart-city programme ($673.75T) runs concurrently with Phase 1, not sequentially — Phase 1's rollout does not delay Phase 2's Year 1–21 timeline. Phase 1 contributes an incremental ~$377.52B/yr rental revenue once complete, ~0.2% of Phase 2's terminal revenue — immaterial to headline figures. Phase 2 financing has been revised: smart city construction is now financed solely through a monthly PAT-Linked MTN Programme (tranches issued only against real, audited profit), superseding the original SBLC-based structure — modeled to complete the full 2,200-city programme in approximately Year 26. Phase 1's 195 separate sovereign guarantees remain active and are NOT superseded — a different mechanism for a different purpose (pilot-country guarantee sizing). See the Delper v10.3 master document, Statement 2 Section V, and the Detailed Project Report, Stage 9, for the corresponding notes.
Structure Choice
PPP vs. FSG, Per Country. FSG (Full Sovereign Guarantee — Delper builds and owns the pilot outright, government only provides a guarantee letter) maximizes Delper's ownership but is capped by each country's guarantee capacity (3% of GDP). PPP (government contributes 25% of construction cost as land/zone, in addition to the guarantee) unlocks a city up to 1.33× larger from the same guarantee capacity — because the government's land contribution funds construction that doesn't need to be guaranteed. The mechanism is close to free for Delper: at 75% ownership of a 1.33×-larger city, Delper's absolute rental revenue is unchanged from a smaller 100%-owned FSG city — but total residents housed rises substantially, and per-city humanitarian impact scales with it.

Tradeoff: Older PPP Yield Basis (7%) — See Note

This PPP-vs-FSG comparison uses the current 13% rental yield applied to a 75%-ownership share, not the older $16.08B/city/yr PPP figure found elsewhere in the v10.3 master document (which was computed at the prior 7% yield and has not been updated). Under the current 13% yield, 75% ownership of a standard city yields approximately $29.86B/city/yr — before the 1.33× size increase PPP enables from the same guarantee capacity, after which Delper's absolute revenue returns to parity with a smaller 100%-owned FSG city. Recommendation: US and China have sufficient guarantee capacity to go straight to FSG; all other 193 pilot countries should be evaluated for PPP structuring to maximize residents served without reducing Delper's guaranteed revenue.

Methodology: each country's guarantee capacity is capped at 3% of nominal GDP (IMF 2026 estimates) — already an aggressive single-project ceiling by sovereign risk standards. Pilot city size (acres, residents) is derived at 95% SBLC loan-to-value using the same per-acre ($61.25M) and per-resident ($122,500) cost ratios as the full Delper programme specification (5,000 acres / 2.5M residents / $306.25B per standard city). Countries whose guarantee capacity exceeds one standard city (United States, China) are capped at the standard city spec, with the multiple noted.

Smart city rental yield is set at 13% p.a. (10% base + 3% profit). Real-world context: prime, stabilized commercial real estate yields typically run 4–8%; higher-risk emerging-market or value-add real estate runs 8–12%. 13% sits above even that higher-risk tier — plausible for an asset explicitly priced as opportunistic-risk, but in tension with the sovereign-guaranteed, low-risk framing used elsewhere in this programme for the same asset.

As established in the Delper v10.3 Reconciliation Notes: shrinking the city to fit guarantee capacity does not resolve project-level debt service coverage on its own — DSCR (NOI ÷ debt service) is scale-invariant and, at the smart city rental yield now set to 13% p.a. (10% base + 3% profit), returns approximately 2.01× regardless of city size, for every country below — clearing both the 1.10× FSG-reduced and 1.25× commercial covenants. (At the prior 7% yield, this figure was 1.08× and fell short of covenant.) This table addresses guarantee-size feasibility only, not project bankability. Population and GDP figures are the latest available UN Population Division and IMF World Economic Outlook (April 2026) estimates and are illustrative; no independent verification has been performed for this table.

Running the pilot in every country on Earth, each sized to its own realistic guarantee capacity, totals $2.904T in construction cost — 0.43% of the full 2,200-city programme's $673.75T. Combined rental revenue at the 13% yield (10% base + 3% profit) is $377.52B/yr, up from $203.28B/yr at the prior 7% yield. Aggregate DSCR across all 195 pilots combined is 2.01×, identical to the per-country figure — pooling doesn't change it either way, since every pilot shares the same underlying yield, LTV, rate, and tenor ratios. At 13%, rental income alone now clears both the 1.10× FSG-reduced and 1.25× commercial covenants without requiring the revenue-pledge mechanism needed at the prior 7% yield.
